Automated Lighting Control with Arduino & Sensors

Engineered a smart lighting control system using Arduino Uno, C/C++ programming, PIR sensors, LDR, and relay modules to automate corridor lighting at FUTA. The embedded system uses sensor-based logic to detect motion and ambient light levels, activating seven 12W LED lamps only when needed. Simulated with Wokwi and tested on-site, it achieved 83.33% energy savings. The IoT-based solution improves energy efficiency and security, with ₦760,233.60 projected annual savings and a 1.14-year return on investment.
